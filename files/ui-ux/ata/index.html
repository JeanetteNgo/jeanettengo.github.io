Aureus Trial Adventure

Duration Mid Feb 2023 - End Mar 2023
My Role UI/UX Designer
Tools Used Figma, Figjam, LucidChart

Introduction

Aureus Academy is a leading music school in Singapore, with over 18 outlets offering tailored one-on-one music lessons for students of all ages and skill levels. Prospective students are able to sign up for a free trial lesson before committing to regular music lessons.

The Aureus Trial Adventure (ATA) is an initiative to digitalise and elevate the current classroom experience for trial music lessons. v1.0.0 was released in April 2023 as a minimum viable product, featuring a set of basic but essential screens to help teachers better conduct their lessons in class.

My Role

    As the lead UI/UX designer, I:
  • Worked collaboratively with the team to understand and chart out lesson flows
  • Designed UI elements and accompanying interactions for all screens
  • Created a functioning prototype for a product demo
  • Liased with graphic illustrators, motion graphic designers and audio producers for the necessary assets. Managed the consolidation of graphic assets across team functions, resulting in a more streamlined content upload process.
  • Took charge of design QA by reporting any discrepancies between what was built versus what was designed

Objective and Process

Before diving in, the team invited a training manager who is familiar with on-the-ground activities to give us a walkthrough of the current trial lesson experience. We learned that in a single trial lesson, teachers had to switch back and forth between multiple mediums such as videos, printed booklets, and external websites, according to internal curriculum requirements.

After gaining a better understanding, the objective of this project thus became to streamline the classroom trial experience. Instead of using physical activity booklets, teachers can now utilize the ATA app to conduct their lessons. The target audience being: young students aged 3-6 years old (major demographic) whose parents have signed them up for a trial music lesson with Aureus.

Design & Development

Given the short timeline, we optimised the build process by constraining the designs to a set of 7 reusable layouts to facilitate the ease of development and content management.

Activity Introduction

The info bottom layout appears at the start of every milestone activity, and features a different character at each milestone to introduce the upcoming activity to the student.



Teaching with Carousels

We needed a fun way to display a series of informational or instructional screens, and that's where the "carousel" layout comes into play.

The carousel layout is distinguished by its left and right arrows, which students can use to navigate to the previous or next in sequence in a series of related screens. This breaks up the monotony of having to tap on the 'Next' button multiple times in a row whenever instructional screens (which young students usually associate with the word "boring"!) come up.



Engaging with Audio

The Aureus team places great importance on creating immersive and engaging music experiences. The "audio card" layout features a few of our characters in their natural habitats, along with types of sounds that the students have to identity upon tapping on each card.



Learning with Videos

What good is theory without practice? After going through the necessary know-hows, students are given the chance to hone their practical skills by picking a song of their choice to play along.



"Great Job" Pop-ups

A little encouragement goes a long way. The popup layout is used for congratulatory messages that appear upon completion of a milestone activity. On top of providing segmentation in the flow, serving as an indicator that the current stage has ended and the next stage is about to begin, these pop-ups invoke in students a sense of achievement and fullfilment.



Report Card

At the end of their journey, teachers can evaluate the student based on their performance in various categories. A report card will be generated for each student as a keepsake of their experience with Aureus.


Concluding Thoughts

Borrowing from gamification theories, the team was able to experiment and create an engaging musical experience for young students dipping their toes into music for the first time.

The project concluded as a successful trial run with positive engagement and feedback from teachers, students and parents. This has kickstarted plans to expand on the project to encompass not only trial lessons, but also normal lessons at Aureus Academy.
