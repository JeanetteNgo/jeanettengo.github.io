☲ Shopee UI/UX Case Study ☴

Duration Dec 2020
Project Type Group (4 members)
Role UI/UX Designer
Tools Used Miro, Figma

Overview

This was originally part of a project submission for the Shopee Product & Design Challenge 2021. Participants had about 12 days from the release of the design brief to conceptualize and come up with ideas to improve the current Shopee app. Now, unconstrained by a deadline and design specifications, I have decided to expand on the ideas my team came up with, but you can check out our original submission on Behance! 🤩

Introduction

Launched in 2015, Shopee has grown to become one of the leading e-commerce platforms in Southeast Asia and Taiwan. It boasts a wide selection of products, and offers an easy, secure, and fast online shopping experience for consumers.

User Research

To gain a deeper understanding of user needs, my team administered a survey to a sample size of 39 respondents, asking questions relating to their usage of e-commerce applications. Our sample focuses on Shopee’s target user group, mostly consisting of young adults in the 21-30 years old range.

    The survey sought to:
  • understand the average user behaviour and interactions with the Shopee app;
  • find out why users might (or might not) prefer the Shopee app over other major e-commerce competitors (such as Lazada and AliExpress);
  • identify common user pain points.

We discovered most survey respondents to be casual browsers, spending on average less than 30 minutes on the app each time.
Some of the reasons cited for using Shopee include: convenience, low prices, and abundance of good deals. However, users also identified a few issues they were frustrated with: namely, the lack of reviews for products, lack of ratings for sellers, and a cluttered app interface.

User Persona

Based on the key findings gathered from our user research, I created 2 personas depicting a user’s behaviour and pain-points when using the app.

Problem Definition

  1. Overly cluttered homepage
  2. Excessive ad banners interfering with browsing experience
  3. Too many different features and sections with no clear distinctions between them
  4. Lack of product ratings and seller feedback

These pain points contribute to cognitive overload when a user first arrives at the homepage, overcomplicates the browsing process, and makes it harder for the user to make a purchase decision due to insufficient product reviews.

Proposed Improvements

  1. A more personalized homepage containing only the most important features and listings
  2. Strategically placed banners and pop-ups that do not overly intrude on a user’s browsing experience
  3. More user-friendly layout and neater categorization of features overall
  4. Rework reminder for users to leave product ratings and seller reviews

High Fidelity Prototyping

Armed with all of our research and brainstormed ideas, we then moved on to design a high fidelity version of our prototype on Figma.

Home Screen & Pop-up feature

The original homescreen felt overly cluttered with advertisements, multiple quick access tabs, and was inundated with various item listings hailing from different segments such as: "Fresh Deals Daily, "Flash Deals", "Top Products", "Shopee Mall", "Dining & Services", "Trending Searches", "Featured Collections", "Categories", and "Daily Discover" ← Yeah that was hard to digest

In my design, I decided to retain only select segments which I thought were relevant and belonged to the main feed, such as a consolidated section for banner ads, shopeepay wallet and coins preview, quick access tabs, "Flash Deals" and "Daily Discover".

A new function proposed here is the pop-up overlay, which would allow the user to conveniently view key details about a product such as product photos, name, price, rating and number sold, as well as seller ratings, and to easily favourite or add the product to cart. According to our research data, these factors play a prominent role in determining whether a user ends up purchasing a product. Thus, providing users the option to quickly browse products they fancy, acquire relevant details, and add them to cart could potentially drive up sales.

Search

New search interface allows users to easily search previously "liked" items.

Categories

The original categories section was inconspicuous and hard to access, being buried amongst other sections in the home page, requiring users to scroll several times to access it.

The new design introduces a dedicated categories section that is comparatively more visible and easily accessible via the top left corner of the homescreen.

Notifications

A more streamlined notification channel, combining the previously separate chat and notifications icons.

Entertainment

Better categorization and accessibility by incorporating Shopee Games, Shopee Feed and Shopee Live into a single "Entertainment" tab.

Shopee Mall

Clearer distinction between Shopee Mall and other Shopee items, for those confused on what Shopee Mall is.

Profile & Rating

Improved rating system, allowing users to set a reminder prompt to rate a product later should they wish to test out a product for a certain period first.
