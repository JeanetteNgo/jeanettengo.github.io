DBS Paylah!

Duration Aug - Sep 2021
Project Type Group (4 members)
Role UI/UX Designer
Tools Used Miro, Figma

Overview

This is a student project submission for my NM3221 Mobile Interaction Design module.

Design Brief

Do a case-study analysis of an existing mobile/web asset, taking into account the stakeholders involved, surveying of user opinions on the current design being used, and proposing a re-design on a specific pain point that improves on issues that the current mobile/web asset has.

Introduction

DBS PayLah! was first introduced in 2014, admist the drive towards cashless payment in Singapore as part of the Smart Nation Initiative. Today, digital wallets are the second-most popular payment method in Singapore, with many competitors in the market such as FavePay, GrabPay and Google Pay. Reports have also shown that usage of digital wallets in e-commerce is expected to rise exponentially in the coming years.

User Research

To gain a deeper understanding of user needs, my team administered a survey to a sample size of 39 respondents, asking questions relating to their usage of e-commerce applications. Our sample focuses on Shopee’s target user group, mostly consisting of young adults in the 21-30 years old range.

    The survey sought to:
  • understand the average user behaviour and interactions with the Shopee app;
  • find out why users might (or might not) prefer the Shopee app over other major e-commerce competitors (such as Lazada and AliExpress);
  • identify common user pain points.

We discovered most survey respondents to be casual browsers, spending on average less than 30 minutes on the app each time.
Some of the reasons cited for using Shopee include:
👍 convenience, low prices, and abundance of good deals.

However, users also identified a few issues they were frustrated with, namely:
👎 lack of reviews for products, lack of ratings for sellers, and a cluttered app interface.

User Persona

Based on the key findings gathered from our user research, I created 2 personas depicting a user’s behaviour and pain-points when using the app.

Problem Definition

  1. Overly cluttered homepage
  2. Excessive ad banners interfering with browsing experience
  3. Too many different features and sections with no clear distinctions between them
  4. Lack of product ratings and seller feedback

These pain points contribute to cognitive overload when a user first arrives at the homepage, overcomplicates the browsing process, and makes it harder for the user to make a purchase decision due to insufficient product reviews.

Proposed Improvements

  1. A more personalized homepage containing only the most important features and listings
  2. Strategically placed banners and pop-ups that do not overly intrude on a user’s browsing experience
  3. More user-friendly layout and neater categorization of features overall
  4. Rework reminder for users to leave product ratings and seller reviews
